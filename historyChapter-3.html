📘 Dr. J.B. Danquah & The Gold Coast Youth Conference

👤 Who was Dr. Joseph Boakye Danquah?

Dr. Joseph Boakye Danquah (1895–1965) was one of Ghana's most important founding fathers. He was a brilliant scholar, lawyer, politician, and traditional ruler. He is widely remembered as the "Doyen of Gold Coast Politics" — meaning the senior and most respected leader of Gold Coast politics in his time.

💡 Introduction: The Young Educated Elites

By the late 1920s, a new generation of young, educated Africans had emerged in the Gold Coast. These young people had gone to school, some even abroad, and they were eager to see their country develop. They wanted a greater say in how the country was governed. This is when the Gold Coast Youth Conference (GCYC) was born.

💭 Think About It: Why do you think young, educated people would want to form their own conference? What unique contributions could young people bring to the struggle for independence?

📜 The Gold Coast Youth Conference (GCYC) — 1929

The Gold Coast Youth Conference was formed in 1929 by Dr. J.B. Danquah together with Joseph Ephraim Casely Hayford (whom we learned about in Week 3). The GCYC was different from earlier protest movements because it was led mainly by young educated elites.

Main Aim of the GCYC:

  • To encourage the constitutional and economic development of the Gold Coast.
  • To bring the educated elites and the traditional chiefs together to work as one team.
  • To educate the public about their rights and the need for self-government.

🎓 Dr. J.B. Danquah's Leadership (1937–1947)

Dr. J.B. Danquah became the main leader of the GCYC from 1937 until 1947. During his leadership, he played several important roles:

  • Uniting Leaders: His main role was to bring the educated elites and the traditional chiefs together so they could work as one force for change.
  • Public Education: Through conferences and lectures, the GCYC educated many public servants, traders, and school leavers about their rights.
  • Constitutional Reforms: The GCYC designed and presented a programme for constitutional reforms — meaning they wrote down the changes they wanted in the country's laws.
  • University Development: The GCYC played an important role in the establishment of the University College of the Gold Coast (now the University of Ghana, Legon).

🏛️ Achievements of the GCYC

1929
Formation: GCYC was founded by Dr. J.B. Danquah and Casely Hayford to promote constitutional and economic development.
1930s
Education Campaign: Through conferences and lectures, the GCYC educated thousands of public servants, traders, and school leavers.
1940s
University Role: The GCYC played an important role in establishing the University College of the Gold Coast.
1947
UGCC Formation: Danquah and other GCYC members helped form the United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC), the first political party demanding immediate self-government.

⭐ The Big Six — Ghana's Founding Fathers

Dr. J.B. Danquah was one of "The Big Six" — the six founding fathers of Ghana who were arrested by the British colonial government after the 1948 riots. The Big Six were:

🌟 Dr. J.B. Danquah
Dr. Kwame Nkrumah
Edward Akufo-Addo
Emmanuel Obetsebi-Lamptey
Dr. Ebenezer Ako-Adjei
William Ofori Atta

These six leaders were members of the UGCC (United Gold Coast Convention), which was formed in 1947 with Danquah's help. The UGCC was the first major political party in the Gold Coast that demanded immediate self-government.

🏛️ Danquah Circle — A Lasting Legacy

If you have ever been to Accra, you may have heard of Danquah Circle — a major junction in the centre of Accra. This important place is named after Dr. J.B. Danquah to honour his contribution to Ghana's independence. It reminds us every day of the sacrifices made by our founding fathers.

Other achievements of Dr. J.B. Danquah:

  • He was elected as a Member of Parliament (MP) in 1951.
  • He was a brilliant scholar who studied in the United Kingdom.
  • He helped draft the ideas that eventually led to Ghana's independence in 1957.
  • He is remembered as a teacher to many other independence leaders, including Kwame Nkrumah.
💭 Think About It: Dr. J.B. Danquah brought together the educated elites and the traditional chiefs. Why do you think it was important for these two groups to work together? What would have happened if they had not united?

  • Danquah led the GCYC from 1937 to 1947.
  • The main aim was constitutional and economic development of the Gold Coast.

  • Danquah was one of The Big Six founding fathers of Ghana.
  • Danquah Circle in Accra is named in his honour.
