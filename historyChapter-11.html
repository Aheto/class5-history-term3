📊 Assessment Overview

Welcome to the End of Term Assessment! This assessment covers everything you've learned in Term 3 about Ghana's journey to independence. You will be tested on:

  • Early protest movements (ARPS, NCBWA, GCYC)
  • Key personalities and their roles
  • Post-WWII conditions and unhappiness
  • The boycott of European goods
  • The shooting of ex-servicemen on 28th February 1948
  • The 1948 riots and arrest of the Big Six
  • The Watson Commission and Coussey Committee

📚 Term 3 Content Review

Before taking the assessment, let's review the key topics from Term 3:

Weeks 1-4: Early Protest Movements

  • ARPS (1897): Formed to protect African land rights against Land Bills. Led by Joseph Mensah Sarbah.
  • NCBWA (1917): Brought together four British West African colonies. Led by Joseph Ephraim Casely Hayford.
  • GCYC (1929): Led by young educated elites including Dr. J.B. Danquah to encourage constitutional and economic development.

Weeks 5-8: Post-WWII Unrest & The 1948 Riots

  • Post-WWII Conditions: Broken promises to soldiers, high prices, unemployment, shortage of goods.
  • Boycott (January 1948): Led by Chief Nii Kwabena Bonne III against European goods with inflated prices.
  • 28th February 1948: Unarmed ex-servicemen marching to Christiansborg Castle were shot. Three died: Sgt. Adjetey, Cpl. Attipoe, Pte. Odartey Lamptey.
  • The Riots: Spread throughout the country. The Big Six were arrested.

Weeks 9-12: Commissions & Constitutional Reform

  • Watson Commission: Investigated causes of riots. Recommended that locals draft their own constitution.
  • Coussey Committee: Established to review and draft new constitution with more African participation.
  • Path to Independence: These events set the stage for Ghana's independence in 1957.
💭 Think About It: How did the early protest movements lay the foundation for Ghana's independence? What lessons can we learn from the bravery of those who fought for our freedom?

🎓 Tips for Success

  • For essay questions, organize your thoughts before writing.
  • Use specific dates, names, and events to support your answers.
  • Take your time — this assessment covers 12 weeks of learning!
