Term 3 — Master Lesson Plan & Notes

NaCCA Standards-Based Curriculum

Term 3 Learning Objectives

Explore Ghana's journey to independence through early protest movements, post-WWII unrest, and the pivotal 1948 riots across four thematic blocks.

Weeks 1-3 B5.5.1.1.1 - B5.5.1.1.2

Early Protest Movements & ARPS

Strand: Journey to Independence  |  Sub-strand: Early Protest Movements before 1945

By the end of these lessons, learners will be able to:

  • Define what a protest movement is and why people form them
  • Identify the three main early protest movements: ARPS (1897), NCBWA (1917), and GCYC (1929)
  • Discuss the sequence of events that led to the formation of these movements
  • Examine the role of Joseph Mensah Sarbah in the Aborigines' Rights Protection Society
Core Competencies: Critical Thinking & Problem Solving, Cultural Identity & Global Citizenship
Protest MovementsARPSLand BillsJoseph Mensah SarbahLegislative Council
Weeks 4-6 B5.5.1.1.3 - B5.5.3.1.1

NCBWA, GCYC & Post-WWII Unrest

Strand: Journey to Independence  |  Sub-strand: Key Leaders & Post-War Conditions

By the end of these lessons, learners will be able to:

  • Narrate the role of Joseph Ephraim Casely Hayford in the NCBWA
  • Present a narrative of Dr. J.B. Danquah's role in the Gold Coast Youth Conference
  • Explain why people were unhappy in the country after the Second World War
  • Describe the broken promises made to Gold Coast soldiers who fought in WWII
Core Competencies: Communication & Collaboration, Creativity & Innovation
Casely HayfordJ.B. DanquahNCBWAGCYCPost-WWIIUnrest
Weeks 7-9 B5.5.3.1.1 - B5.5.3.1.2

The Boycott & The 1948 Riots

Strand: Journey to Independence  |  Sub-strand: The 1948 Riots

By the end of these lessons, learners will be able to:

  • Explain why people boycotted European goods and how the boycott was organized
  • Describe the events of 28th February 1948 when unarmed ex-servicemen were shot
  • Identify the three ex-servicemen: Sgt. Adjetey, Cpl. Attipoe, and Pte. Odartey Lamptey
  • Examine sources of evidence about what happened during the 1948 riots and the arrest of the Big Six
Core Competencies: Critical Thinking & Problem Solving, Personal Development & Leadership
Boycott28th February 1948Ex-ServicemenBig SixChristiansborg Castle
Weeks 10-12 B5.5.3.1.2 - B5.5.3.1.3

Watson & Coussey Commissions

Strand: Journey to Independence  |  Sub-strand: Post-Riot Commissions & Constitutional Reform

By the end of these lessons, learners will be able to:

  • Explain what the Watson Commission was and describe its key findings
  • Discuss the recommendations of the Watson Commission for constitutional reform
  • Explain why the Coussey Committee was established and its main recommendations
  • Trace the sequence of events from the 1948 riots to the path toward independence
Core Competencies: Communication & Collaboration, Cultural Identity & Global Citizenship

Differentiation Strategies

Tailored support to ensure every learner succeeds — whether they need extra help or an extra challenge.

For Struggling Learners

  • Provide simplified texts, sentence frames, and extra time for research and writing tasks
  • Use visual aids, timelines, and anchor charts for key personalities and events
  • Pair learners with able peers in group work; assign specific roles (scribe, researcher, designer, orator)
  • Offer flow chart templates and guided notes for complex sequences like the 1948 riots

For Advanced Learners

  • Challenge learners to write longer essays (150-180 words) on key personalities like Casely Hayford
  • Assign leadership roles in group research, documentary creation, and debate activities
  • Encourage deeper research using internet sources and library books on "Ethiopia Unbound"
  • Extend tasks to include comparative analysis of different protest movements and commissions
