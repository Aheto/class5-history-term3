The Coussey Committee

B5.5.3.1.3 • Journey to Independence
Student View

🎯 What You'll Learn

  • Explain why the Coussey Committee was established following the Watson Commission.
  • Describe the main recommendations of the Coussey Committee for constitutional reform.
  • Discuss the impact of the Coussey Committee on Ghana's constitution and path to self-government.
  • Trace the sequence of events from the 1948 riots to the path toward independence.

📘 The Coussey Committee

💡 Introduction: From Riots to Reform

After the 1948 riots and the Watson Commission's report, the British colonial government realised that major changes were needed. The Watson Commission recommended that a committee be set up to review the constitution of the Gold Coast and draft a new one. This led to the establishment of the Coussey Committee in 1949.

💭 Think About It: Why do you think the British government decided to set up a committee instead of just making changes themselves? What does this tell us about the power of the 1948 riots?

📜 Why Was the Coussey Committee Established?

The Coussey Committee was established because:

  • The Watson Commission recommended that local people should be allowed to draft their own constitution
  • The 1948 riots showed that Africans were demanding more participation in their own government
  • The existing constitution did not give Africans enough representation or power
  • There was a need for constitutional reforms to move toward self-government

👥 Who Was on the Coussey Committee?

The committee was chaired by Mr. Justice Coussey, a judge. It included both African and European members, but for the first time, Africans were in the majority. This was a significant step because it meant Africans would have more say in drafting the new constitution.

📋 Main Recommendations of the Coussey Committee

The Coussey Committee made several important recommendations:

  • More Elected African Members: The Legislative Council should have more elected African members instead of appointed ones
  • Greater African Participation: Africans should have greater participation in government at all levels
  • Executive Council Reform: The Executive Council should include more African members with real responsibilities
  • Local Government: Strengthen local government structures to give more power to local communities
  • Constitutional Framework: Create a new constitution that would lead to self-government

🏛️ Impact on Ghana's Constitution

The Coussey Committee's recommendations led to the 1951 Constitution, which was a major milestone in Ghana's journey to independence:

  • For the first time, Africans had a majority in the Legislative Council
  • The first general elections were held in 1951
  • Dr. Kwame Nkrumah and his Convention People's Party (CPP) won the elections
  • Nkrumah became the Leader of Government Business, marking the beginning of self-government

🌍 Path to Self-Government and Independence

The Coussey Committee was a crucial stepping stone on the path to independence:

1948
The 1948 Riots — Ex-servicemen shot, riots spread, Big Six arrested
1948
Watson Commission — Investigated causes of riots, recommended new constitution
1949
Coussey Committee — Drafted new constitution with African majority
1951
New Constitution & Elections — Africans win majority, Nkrumah becomes Leader of Government Business
1957
Independence — Ghana becomes the first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence

🎯 Why Was the Coussey Committee Important?

The Coussey Committee was important because:

  • It was the first time Africans played a major role in drafting their own constitution
  • It led to the 1951 Constitution which gave Africans majority representation
  • It set the stage for the first democratic elections in 1951
  • It marked the beginning of self-government, which eventually led to full independence in 1957
  • It showed that peaceful constitutional reform could achieve what riots and protests started
💭 Think About It: If you were a member of the Coussey Committee, what would you include in the new constitution to make sure Africans had real power in their own government?
