The Watson Commission

B5.5.3.1.2 • Journey to Independence

  • Understand how the Watson Commission shaped Ghana's path toward independence.

📘 The Watson Commission

💡 What Happened Before the Watson Commission?

After the 1948 riots that shook the Gold Coast, the British colonial government realised that something serious had happened. The shooting of the three ex-servicemen — Sergeant Adjetey, Corporal Attipoe, and Private Odartey Lamptey — on 28th February 1948 had sparked anger across the country. People were rioting, foreign-owned shops were being looted, and the "Big Six" leaders had been arrested. The British government knew they had to find out what had caused all this trouble.

💭 Think About It: If you were the British governor, what would you do after such serious riots broke out across the country?

🏛️ What Was the Watson Commission?

The Watson Commission was a team of investigators sent by the British colonial government to the Gold Coast in 1948. It was named after its chairman, Mr. A. C. Watson. The commission was given a very important job:

  • To investigate (find out) what had caused the 1948 riots
  • To look at the general state of affairs in the Gold Coast
  • To make recommendations (suggestions) to the British government on how to respond

A commission like this is a group of people chosen to look carefully into a serious problem and report their findings.

🔍 The Main Findings of the Watson Commission

After listening to many people and studying the situation, the Watson Commission discovered several serious problems that had caused the unrest:

📋 Key Findings:
  1. Broken Promises: The British had not kept their promises to the ex-servicemen who fought in World War II — no pensions, no jobs, no housing.
  2. High Prices & Shortage of Goods: Trading companies had increased prices of imported goods, making life very hard for ordinary people.
  3. Lack of Jobs: Returning soldiers and young educated people could not find work.
  4. Foreign Control of the Economy: Foreign companies controlled trade and resources, leaving Africans poor.
  5. Lack of African Representation: Africans had very little say in how the country was governed through the Legislative Council.
  6. Distance Between Government and People: The colonial government did not listen to the complaints of ordinary Ghanaians.

💡 The Recommendations of the Watson Commission

The Watson Commission made several important recommendations to the British government. These recommendations were very significant because they recognised that the Gold Coast needed serious changes:

✅ Key Recommendations:
  1. New Constitution: Local people (Ghanaians) should be allowed to draft their own Constitution.
  2. More Africans in Government: More Africans should be included in the government and decision-making processes.
  3. Constitutional Reforms: The political system needed to be changed to give Africans more power.
  4. Listen to the People: The government should pay attention to the complaints and needs of ordinary citizens.
  5. Political Education: There should be efforts to educate the public about political matters.

🌍 Why Was the Watson Commission Important?

The Watson Commission was a turning point in Ghana's journey to independence. For the first time, the British government officially admitted that there were serious problems with the way the Gold Coast was being governed. The commission's recommendations led directly to the next important step — the Coussey Committee, which was set up to draft the new constitution that the Watson Commission had recommended.

28th February: Shooting of ex-servicemen → Riots break out → Big Six arrested
1948
Watson Commission: Sent by British government to investigate the riots
1949
Coussey Committee: Set up to draft a new constitution based on Watson's recommendations

Independence: Ghana becomes the first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence
💭 Think About It: The Watson Commission recommended that Ghanaians should draft their own constitution. Why do you think this was such an important recommendation? How is it connected to independence?

📚 Group Activity: The Watson Commission Report

In your groups, imagine you are members of the Watson Commission. Prepare a short report that includes:

  • Three main causes of the 1948 riots
  • Three recommendations you would make to the British government
  • Why you believe these changes are necessary

Present your report to the class. Think about how your recommendations compare to the real Watson Commission's recommendations!
